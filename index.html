Our Encryption: enSed

We use a custom matrix-based encryption library called "enSed" to ensure data is secure and meaningless without the correct decryption key.

Our custom algorithm, enSed, forms the core of our security. Instead of standard methods, we convert text messages into a series of numerical matrices. These matrices are then multiplied by a secret, non-singular matrix key. The resulting matrix is flattened into a string of numbers, which is the encrypted data transmitted by the light.

To decrypt, the receiver uses the mathematical inverse of the original key. Multiplying the received data by this inverse key reverses the process, perfectly restoring the original message. This method ensures that without the exact inverse matrix, the intercepted data remains a meaningless stream of numbers.
